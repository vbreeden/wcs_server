FAQ

What do you mean by "most danceable West Coast Swing?"

I'm looking for the song that, when you're dancing to it, Just. Works. There's no confusion, it doesn't keep you guessing what's going to happen next, and when the song is over you feel like you just had the best dance of the night.

What is the Song URL?

This can be a link to anywhere where I can hear the song; YouTube, Spotify, iTunes, and similar places.

What do I put in the 'Features' box?

What I'm looking for here is to know what you think makes the song such a good West Coast Swing. There are no wrong answers, and how you answer it is completely up to you. If you don't have an answer that's ok too, this field is optional.

What's the reason for the Dancer Information?

This will help me better find patterns that could be related to dancers in a given demographic. Is there something in the music that we dance to that's easier for followers than for leads? Is there a difference in what people from Berlin will find easy to dance to versus people from San Diego? Is there something that these songs that they have in common that's unexpected? These are the types of question I will be asking when analyzing this data.

Will I be able to see the songs that other people chose?

Eventually, but not at first. While I'm collecting data I will keep it confidential to prevent artificial trends from developing.

However! Once I've collected enough data I will make the list of songs and URLs searchable.

If you find anything interesting will you share your findings?

You bet! The whole purpose of this project is to learn new things about the music we dance to and to share those findings with the West Coast Swing community.
